\section{Overview}

All VSEPR-Sim molecular I/O uses plain-text, line-oriented files.
Four formats share a common structural grammar; each adds fields to
the preceding one.

\begin{center}
\small
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\toprule
\textbf{Ext.} & \textbf{Name} & \textbf{Use case}\\
\midrule
\texttt{.xyz}  & Standard XYZ       & Static geometry snapshot \\
\texttt{.xyza} & Extended XYZ       & Charges, velocities, forces \\
\texttt{.xyzc} & Checkpoint XYZ     & Restartable MD state \\
\texttt{.xyzf} & Formation XYZ      & Multi-frame trajectory \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\textbf{Units (all formats):}
coordinates in \AA{} ($10^{-10}$\,m);
energies in kcal/mol;
velocities in \AA/fs;
forces in kcal/(mol\,\AA);
charges in elementary charge $e$.

% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Standard XYZ (\texttt{.xyz})}

\subsection{Grammar}

\begin{lstlisting}[style=xyz]
<N>
<comment>
<sym>  <x>  <y>  <z>
...   (N lines)
\end{lstlisting}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Line 1}: atom count $N$ (integer $\ge 1$)
  \item \textbf{Line 2}: free-text comment (may be empty)
  \item \textbf{Lines 3--}: element symbol + three Cartesian coordinates,
        whitespace-separated
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Coordinate system}
Right-handed Cartesian $(x, y, z)$ in \AA.
Origin is arbitrary; conventional choice is near the centre of mass.
Precision: 6 decimal places $\Rightarrow$ resolution $\approx 0.1$\,pm.

\subsection{Example: water}

\begin{lstlisting}[style=xyz]
3
H2O | E = -6.245 kcal/mol
O   0.000000  0.000000  0.119262
H   0.000000  0.763239 -0.477049
H   0.000000 -0.763239 -0.477049
\end{lstlisting}

\subsection{Comment line convention}
\begin{lstlisting}[style=xyz]
<name> | E = <val> kcal/mol | <notes>
\end{lstlisting}

% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Extended XYZ (\texttt{.xyza})}

Adds per-atom scalar and vector properties after the $(x,y,z)$ fields.

\subsection{Grammar}

\begin{lstlisting}[style=xyz]
<N>
<comment> properties="<list>"
<sym> <x> <y> <z> [<q> <vx> <vy> <vz> <fx> <fy> <fz> <e>]
\end{lstlisting}

The \texttt{properties} key in the comment line declares which optional
columns are present (order is fixed):

\begin{center}
\small
\begin{tabular}{llll}
\toprule
\textbf{Key} & \textbf{Symbol} & \textbf{Unit} & \textbf{Cols}\\
\midrule
\texttt{charge}   & $q_i$                    & $e$              & 1 \\
\texttt{velocity} & $\mathbf{v}_i$           & \AA/fs           & 3 \\
\texttt{force}    & $\mathbf{F}_i$           & kcal/(mol\,\AA)  & 3 \\
\texttt{energy}   & $\varepsilon_i$          & kcal/mol         & 1 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\subsection{Example: H$_2$O with charges and velocities}

\begin{lstlisting}[style=xyz]
3
Water MD frame 1 properties="charge:velocity"
O  0.00  0.00  0.12 -0.834  0.00  0.00  0.00
H  0.00  0.76 -0.48  0.417  0.12  0.08 -0.03
H  0.00 -0.76 -0.48  0.417 -0.12  0.08 -0.03
\end{lstlisting}

% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Checkpoint XYZ (\texttt{.xyzc})}

Extends \texttt{.xyza} with a simulation-state header block required to
restart an interrupted MD run without loss of trajectory continuity.

\subsection{Header block (before atom data)}

\begin{lstlisting}[style=xyz]
CHECKPOINT
step    <integer>
time    <float> fs
dt      <float> fs
T_target <float> K
seed    <integer>
box     <ax> <ay> <az>  (Angstrom, optional PBC)
END_CHECKPOINT
<N>
<comment>
<atom lines with charge+velocity+force>
\end{lstlisting}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{step}: MD step number at checkpoint
  \item \texttt{time}: accumulated simulation time (fs)
  \item \texttt{dt}: integrator timestep (fs)
  \item \texttt{T\_target}: thermostat set-point (K); 0 = NVE
  \item \texttt{seed}: RNG seed for reproducible continuation
  \item \texttt{box}: simulation box vectors; omit for open boundary
\end{itemize}

% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Formation XYZ (\texttt{.xyzf})}

A multi-frame format: one or more standard \texttt{.xyz} or \texttt{.xyza}
blocks concatenated in a single file, separated only by the next atom-count
line.  Used for minimisation traces, NVT trajectories, and reaction paths.

\begin{lstlisting}[style=xyz]
<N>          <- frame 1
<comment>
<atom lines>
<N>          <- frame 2
<comment>
<atom lines>
...
\end{lstlisting}

Each frame is self-contained and independently parseable.
The comment line of frame $k$ typically records the step index and
instantaneous energy:

\begin{lstlisting}[style=xyz]
Step 0 | E = -12.30 kcal/mol | FIRE iter
Step 1 | E = -13.11 kcal/mol | FIRE iter
\end{lstlisting}

% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Bounding Box \& PBC}

When periodic boundary conditions are active, the box geometry is
stored either in the \texttt{.xyzc} header or as a special comment-line
key in any format:

\begin{lstlisting}[style=xyz]
Lattice="ax 0 0  0 ay 0  0 0 az" pbc="T T T"
\end{lstlisting}

Box vectors are in \AA.  \texttt{pbc} flags indicate which axes are
periodic (T) or open (F).  The format is compatible with the ASE
\textit{Extended XYZ} convention.

\section{Implementation}

\begin{center}
\small
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.2}
\begin{tabular}{ll}
\toprule
\textbf{File} & \textbf{Role}\\
\midrule
\texttt{include/io/xyz\_format.hpp}  & Struct definitions, API  \\
\texttt{src/io/xyz\_format.cpp}      & Reader / writer impl.    \\
\texttt{include/thermal/xyzc\_format.hpp} & Checkpoint reader \\
\texttt{src/thermal/xyzc\_format.cpp}     & Checkpoint writer \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

Key reader types: \texttt{XYZReader}, \texttt{XYZAReader}, and
\texttt{XYZWriter} / \texttt{XYZAWriter} in \texttt{namespace vsepr::io}.
Bond auto-detection (\texttt{detect\_bonds}) uses per-element covalent radii
with a configurable scale factor (default 1.2).
